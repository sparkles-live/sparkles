Section 3

Methodology

A clean methodology summary highlights the simulation constraints, observational geometry, and the modeling approach used to protect space-based astronomy from satellite contamination.

  • Zenith angle constraints

    Simulations restrict observations to zenith angles below 70° to reduce stray satellite trail exposure near the horizon.

  • Earth avoidance

    Model enforces Earth limb avoidance to minimize scattered sunlight and atmospheric glow in space telescope fields of view.

  • Trail density sampling

    Density maps are generated across latitude, inclination, and local solar time to capture the full exposure risk for each mission.

  • Time-domain validation

    The methodology includes temporal forecast windows and matches simulations to latest satellite catalog ephemerides.

Section 4

Additional Information

Accessible data and software details for the SPARKLES repository, published as an interactive resource reference.

Q: How often are forecasts updated?
A: Daily, with hourly refreshes when new satellite data are available.

Q: Can I import these data into mission planning tools?
A: Yes, model exports support FITS, CSV, and JSON formats for mission integration.

  • FITS: Primary standard for astronomical images and metadata packaging.
  • CSV: Tabular export for schedule planning, trail counts, and model parameters.
  • JSON: Machine-readable catalogs for integration with simulation pipelines.
  • Python 3.10+ for analysis scripts and orbit propagation.
  • Astropy for FITS file handling and celestial coordinate transforms.
  • NumPy / Pandas for data preparation and model evaluation.
  • Jupyter Lab for interactive results exploration and documentation.

Section 6

About SPARKLES

SPARKLES protects the dark sky by quantifying satellite trail impact and giving scientific teams tools to make responsible observation decisions in the era of megaconstellations.

  • Preserve space-based astronomy through predictive trail modeling, mission planning, and stakeholder collaboration.
  • Deliver accessible datasets, open formats, and community-ready science communication.
  • Support policy, operator engagement, and mission design with evidence-based contamination forecasts.

Mission statement

SPARKLES exists to ensure that the next generation of space telescopes can observe the universe with minimal interference from satellite megaconstellations. We bridge the gap between orbital engineering, astronomical science, and data stewardship to defend the integrity of space-based observations.

Core values

  • Science-first decision making
  • Open, reproducible forecasting
  • Long-term dark sky protection
